# The 2am Prayer
**Subtitle:** Finding God When You Can't Sleep

## Topic
A short devotional booklet for Christians who wake at 2am with anxiety, grief, or spiritual restlessness. Helps the reader turn sleepless nights into prayer encounters with a God who keeps watch when the body cannot.

## Target Audience
Christians aged 25-60 who wake between 2am and 4am unable to fall back asleep. Familiar with their Bible but not theologically trained. Carrying weight — financial stress, family grief, health scares, unspoken fear.

## Size Tier
booklet

## Chapter Count
3

## Total Target Length
2500 words (booklet)

## Voice Profile
spiritual-default

## Key Themes
- God's wakefulness vs. our wakefulness (Psalm 121:4 — "he who keeps you will not slumber")
- Fear at 2am is not faithlessness — it is honesty
- The sleepless hour as invitation, not punishment

## Proposed Chapters
1. **The Clock Says 2:17** — The honest moment of waking. Scene: bedroom, phone glow, racing heart. What the body knows that the mind resists. Sets up: fear is not the enemy of faith; pretending is.
2. **The Kitchen Lamp** — Getting up. The walk to the kitchen. Lighting one small lamp. The physical act of refusing to lie in the dark any longer as a parable for surrender. Scripture: Psalm 139:11-12.
3. **The God Who Keeps Watch** — Return to bed or stay at the window. The shift from anxiety to company. Psalm 121. Ending: a written 2am prayer the reader can use next time.

## Source Material
None. This is a topic brief, not a sermon-to-book conversion. The outliner runs in "generate from topic" mode.

## Craft Anchors
- Time markers: "2:17am", "3:04am", "4:42am" — each chapter opens at a slightly different hour to show the long night.
- Sensory anchors: bedroom dark + phone glow (ch1), kitchen lamp + cold tile floor (ch2), window + first grey light (ch3).
- Central image: one small lamp refusing the whole dark — appears in opening 200 words, middle third, and closing 200 words of every chapter.
- Vulnerability beats: author's own named 2am moments (voice profile Reader Moments section is the provenance source).
- Reader moments (≥2 per chapter, from spiritual-default Reader Moments): "the 2am phone-check", "the kitchen at 3am", "the bedside lamp click", "reading the same verse you read last night".

## What This Brief Is NOT
- Not a sleep-science book (no health claims)
- Not a Greek/Hebrew study (transliterated terms strictly capped across the whole booklet)
- Not a sermon (no pulpit-seam phrasing)
