  <action>
    Add a new subsection titled "Revision Cap and Divergent-Improvement Detection (CRAFT-17)" inside the Stage 4 revision workflow section (or inside Mode 3 Revision, wherever the existing chapter revision loop is defined in orchestrator SKILL.md).

    Content:

    **State tracked per chapter during revision:**
    - `revision_count`: integer starting at 0, incremented per revision pass.
    - `revision_history`: list of objects `{revision_n, captivation_total, component_scores, craft_check_failures}` — one entry per revision.

    **Hard cap:** `revision_count` must never exceed 2. After the second revision, no further revision is attempted even if checks still fail.

    **Divergent-improvement detection (after each revision N, N ≥ 1):**
    1. Compare revision N's `component_scores` (7 captivation components) against revision N-1's component_scores.
    2. If revision N scores LOWER than revision N-1 on ANY sub-component (not just the total), declare divergent improvement.
    3. On divergent improvement: accept revision N-1 (keep it as the final), stop the revision loop, append a flag to the diagnostic report: `Chapter N: divergent improvement detected at revision N. Accepted revision N-1 (component X dropped from A to B).`

    **Revision exhaustion handling (2-revision cap hit):**
    - If `revision_count == 2` and checks still fail, select the revision with the highest `captivation_total` (ties broken by lowest total craft_check failures, then by revision_n ascending).
    - Keep that revision as the final `edited/ch[NN]-final.md`.
    - Append flag to diagnostic report: `Chapter N hit the 2-revision cap. Accepted revision {M} (captivation total {T}, craft check failures {F}). Human review recommended at Stage 4 review gate.`
    - Continue the pipeline. No halt. User sees flags at the Stage 4 review gate.

    **Auto-revise trigger integration:**
    - Auto-revise is triggered by editor Pass 1 on CRAFT-01/02/05 failures and by editor Pass 2 §3.3 on scene-first strictness failures.
    - Each auto-revise call goes through this revision loop and increments `revision_count`.
    - Flag-only failures (CRAFT-03/04/06/07/08) do NOT trigger revision; they only append to the diagnostic report.

    Also: ensure the orchestrator's per-chapter state in `reports/consistency-report.md` (or a new `reports/revision-log.md`) records revision_count and revision_history so Stage 4 can show the user exactly what happened.
  </action>
