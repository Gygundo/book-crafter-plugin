**Section 1: Overview**
- Purpose: Transform spoken-rhythm source material into written-rhythm prose before the outliner runs
- Input: Project directory path (received from orchestrator via `$ARGUMENTS`), reads files from `[project_directory]/sources/`
- Output: Adapted files written to `[project_directory]/sources-adapted/`
- Prerequisites: `sources/` directory exists with `.md` files

**Section 2: Sermon Detection Heuristics**

When the orchestrator calls this skill, it has already detected sermon indicators. However, the adapter should also confirm by scanning source files for these patterns:

- ALL CAPS headings (e.g., `POINT 1:`, `THE FOUNDATION OF GRACE`)
- First/second person plural audience address ("we", "us", "you" used as direct address to a congregation)
- Verbal cues: "Let me tell you", "Think about this", "Watch this", "Here's what I love about this", "Now look at this", "Are you tracking with me?"
- Temporal references: "this morning", "last Sunday", "tonight", "this week"
- Spatial references: "here in this room", "in our church", "as we gather"
- Relational references: "as your pastor", "I've told you before"
- Block-quoted scripture followed by commentary pattern ("Now look at what this says...")
- Numbered point lists with sermon-style headings

If fewer than 3 indicators found across all source files, warn: "These source files don't appear to be sermon transcripts. The adapter will still process them, but the output may not differ significantly from the originals."

**Section 3: Transformation Rules**

Apply these seven transformations to each source file, in order:

**Rule 1: Fragment Completion**
- Input pattern: Short fragments, incomplete sentences, one-word emphatic statements
- Transform: Complete into full sentences that preserve the emphasis without relying on spoken delivery
- Example:
  - Before: `Grace. It's not what you think. Not a license. Not a safety net. It's the FOUNDATION.`
  - After: `Grace is not what most people think it is. It is neither a licence to sin nor a safety net for when things go wrong. It is the foundation -- the bedrock upon which everything else is built.`

**Rule 2: Audience De-personalisation**
- Input pattern: "Turn to your neighbour", "This morning", "As your pastor", "Here in our church", "Last Sunday we talked about"
- Transform: Remove or universalise. Replace temporal references with timeless framing. Replace spatial references with universal context.
- Example:
  - Before: `I want you to turn to your neighbour and say "I'm not who I used to be." As we've been walking through this series together...`
  - After: `There comes a moment when a truth settles so deeply that it changes how you see yourself. You are not who you used to be.`

**Rule 3: Verbal Cue Replacement**
- Input pattern: "Watch this", "Here's what I love about this", "Now look at this", "Let me tell you something", "Are you tracking with me?"
- Transform: Replace with written transitions that serve the same function (building anticipation, flagging importance) but in prose form.
- Example:
  - Before: `Now watch this. Here's where it gets good. Look at verse 15. Paul says...`
  - After: `This is where Paul's argument takes a decisive turn. In verse 15, he writes...`

**Rule 4: Repetition Consolidation**
- Input pattern: Same idea stated 2-3 times in slightly different words within a paragraph or section
- Transform: Keep the strongest version, enhance with depth (add word study, cross-reference, or implication), discard the echoes. The book version goes DEEPER once instead of WIDER three times.
- Example:
  - Before: `You are seated with Christ. Seated! Not standing in line. Not waiting outside. SEATED. You are IN Christ. Positioned. Established. Settled.`
  - After: `The word Paul uses is "seated" -- past tense, completed action. Not standing in line hoping for an audience. Not waiting outside the throne room. Seated. The Greek word kathizo carries the sense of permanent establishment -- positioned, established, settled in a place of authority. This is not a future promise. It is a present reality.`

**Rule 5: Structural De-numbering**
- Input pattern: Numbered points with ALL CAPS headings (sermon structure)
- Transform: Convert to flowing prose with natural transitions between ideas. Preserve the logical progression but let it flow as narrative, not bullet points.

**Rule 6: Scripture Re-integration**
- Input pattern: Block-quoted scripture followed by "Now look at what this says..."
- Transform: Weave scripture into narrative flow. The verse should emerge as part of the argument, not precede it as a reading assignment.

**Rule 7: Exclamation and Emphasis Normalisation**
- Input pattern: Multiple exclamation marks, ALL CAPS for emphasis, bold/italic overuse
- Transform: Replace with prose-based emphasis. Sentence structure and word choice create emphasis in writing, not formatting.

**Section 4: Processing Workflow**

On invocation:
1. Read all `.md` files from `[project_directory]/sources/` using `ls [project_directory]/sources/*.md`
2. Create `[project_directory]/sources-adapted/` directory: `mkdir -p [project_directory]/sources-adapted`
3. For each source file:
   a. Read the full content
   b. Apply all 7 transformation rules (in order, as a rewriting pass -- not regex substitution)
   c. Write the adapted content to `[project_directory]/sources-adapted/[original-filename]`
   d. Append a metadata comment at the end:
      ```
      <!-- SERMON ADAPTED
      source: [original filename]
      date: [today]
      transforms_applied: fragment-completion, audience-depersonalisation, verbal-cue-replacement, repetition-consolidation, structural-denumbering, scripture-reintegration, emphasis-normalisation
      -->
      ```
4. After all files processed, report: "Sermon adaptation complete: [N] source files transformed and saved to sources-adapted/"

**Section 5: Anti-Patterns**
- Do NOT treat this as find-and-replace. Each transformation requires understanding context. "Let me tell you" cannot simply become "Consider this" -- the entire sentence rhythm must change.
- Do NOT preserve sermon repetition. If the same idea appears 2-3 times restated, consolidate into one deeper treatment.
- Do NOT create a summary or outline. The adapter preserves the full content length (possibly reducing it slightly by consolidation) in written form. It is NOT an abridgement.
- Do NOT change theological content or interpretations. Transform the FORM, not the SUBSTANCE.
- Do NOT add content that was not in the source. The adapter transforms what exists; the researcher skill adds depth later.

**Section 6: Output Format**
- Each adapted file is a clean markdown document
- Preserve any scripture references in their original form (the researcher will handle proper formatting later)
- Maintain the logical flow and argument structure of the original sermon, even though the presentation style changes
- Target: adapted content should read as if it were originally written as a book chapter draft, not transcribed from speech
